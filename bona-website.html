Bona Cafe and Market Website

A web development course project focused on creating the website for Bona Cafe, a local Vancouver business.

Initial Research

Examined precedents from other local businesses in the same space and identified commonalities between each website to incorporate into our design.

  • Large images on the landing page
  • Photo-focused menus
  • Hours and addresses included in the footer

After looking at the research we were able to determine what the goal was for the Bona Cafe website.

Goal

To create a website that is functional, accessible, and responsive while also feeling familiar to users and maintaining a unique style specific to Bona cafe.

What I did

Translating the design files into functional code

As my partner concentrated more on the design aspects, I took the lead in developing the more complex and structured pages. This involved carefully reviewing the design files and asking clarifying questions about layout spacing, component states, and interactive elements. My goal was to ensure that the final website matched the intended visual direction and that everything translated smoothly into code. This approach helped me break the design into modular, manageable pieces for development.

Creating responsive components

I designed the navigation bar and footer shown above, ensuring they adhere to accessibility best practices and responsive design principles for optimal performance across various screens and user demographics.

Troubleshooting and debugging

After critique and feedback sessions, I reviewed the project files to ensure alignment with modern best practices and addressed any issues raised by peers or my instructor. I also resolved debugging problems and handled troubleshooting throughout the project.

Takeaways

Overall, this project taught me a great deal about the design-to-development process and strengthened my ability to collaborate with a designer. I learned the importance of clear communication to ensure that the visual direction is clear and accurately translates onto the web. I also reinforced my ability to code with responsiveness and accessibility at the forefront.
